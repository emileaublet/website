## Challenge

Croesus has led Canadian wealth management for 30+ years. AI is raising the bar on what advisors expect from their tools, and my role in Innovation is to define what comes next, and where advisors still fit in that picture.

The gap was foundational: Croesus had no design system. Every new product or rebuild started from scratch, which meant every team was re-solving the same problems, and nothing was built with AI tooling in mind.

## Approach

I built the system Croesus was missing: accessible, cross-framework, and AI-optimized from day one. It works natively across React and Angular, with coded examples grounded in real wealth-management workflows rather than generic boilerplate. Making it AI-ready also meant more than documenting components for humans. I built docs and structured skills that let AI assistants use the system correctly, not just look it up, a distinction that matters once AI is generating a meaningful share of the UI code.

## Lessons

Building a design system for a 30-year-old platform is a different problem than building one for a startup: the constraint isn't a blank canvas, it's earning trust from teams who've been burned by "the next big rewrite" before. Making Innovation the system's first and most demanding user was what made that trust possible. We felt every gap before anyone else had to.

Optimizing for AI from the start, rather than retrofitting it later, also turned out to matter more than I expected. The teams building on the system today are shipping faster because both their human developers and their AI tooling can rely on the same accessible, well-documented foundation.
